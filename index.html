How to Determine Your Hiking Level?

Choosing the right trail starts with an honest assessment of your physical condition, experience, and gear. Here are a few key criteria to help you:
  • Physical fitness: Can you comfortably hike 5–10 km a day? How well do you handle elevation, heat, or cold?
  • Experience: Have you hiked in the mountains, camped overnight, or navigated unfamiliar terrain?
  • Gear: Do you have proper footwear, clothing, backpack, and a first aid kit?
  • Navigation skills: Can you use GPS, maps, or a compass confidently?
  • Mental readiness: Are you comfortable in remote areas without cell service?
Tip: Start with easy trails and gradually work your way up. Keeping a hiking journal can help track your progress and build confidence.

Useful Resources for Trail Selection

AllTrails

— a user-friendly platform with difficulty ratings, photos, and reviews. Especially helpful for trails in Ukraine.

Modern Hiker

— an English-language resource with detailed guides across the U.S. and beyond, focusing on nature, history, and accessibility.

Want to find out what level suits you best? Pick a trail on AllTrails and compare its rating to your current abilities — it’s the most practical way to test yourself in real conditions.
